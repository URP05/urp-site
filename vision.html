VISION • SHORT DOCUMENT

URP’s vision: a secure, capable and respected Uttarakhand

Our goal is not only to change governments, but to improve the quality of governance. Simply put — “Work should be visible, numbers should be clear and citizens should decide.”

Six-pillar vision (short)

This is a concise framework. The detailed manifesto will be prepared after seat-wise surveys and conversations with citizens.

1. Governance & Transparency

“Out of the file, into public view” — every major project, cost and timeline should be visible to people, not hidden in paperwork.

  • District-wise public portal with project name, budget, contractor, status, photos and location.
  • Quarterly public report — not just speeches, but clear numbers and progress updates.

2. Jobs & Youth Empowerment

“Earn from your own district” — young people should not be forced to leave the state for every job opportunity.

  • Multi-skill hubs in every district — IT, tourism, logistics, agro-processing and digital services.
  • Local job-mapping portal that clearly shows which companies are hiring and where.

3. Women’s Safety & Opportunity

Streets, markets, schools and workplaces must be safe and respectful for women in daily life.

  • Well-lit key areas, CCTV, women’s help-desk and rapid response teams in urban centres.
  • Stronger linkages between women’s self-help groups and tourism, food, crafts and service-based income chains.

4. Health & Education Reset

Hospitals and schools should be judged by outcomes, not just buildings and photo-ops.

  • District hospitals with 24×7 emergency services, specialists and tele-medicine support for hill blocks.
  • Teacher-posting audits, digital classrooms and local-language support in difficult hill schools.

5. Reverse Migration & Revive Villages

Emptying villages should not remain only a matter of nostalgia — they should be revived through new economic models.

  • “Adopt-a-village” style initiatives linking youth, ex-servicemen and the Uttarakhand diaspora to village revival.
  • Eco-tourism, homestays, learning centres and small campuses rooted in mountain life.

6. Environment & Disaster Preparedness

Uttarakhand’s future is tied to its rivers, forests and mountains. Development that ignores this cannot be sustainable.

  • Reviving springs and rivers, mapping landslide-prone zones and preventive work in vulnerable areas.
  • Block-level disaster-ready volunteer networks with regular drills and training.

From vision to policy — how will it be built?

This vision is not a top-down document. It will be refined with people in each district and constituency.

1. Constituency-level surveys

Structured surveys in every assembly seat to capture separate priorities of youth, women, farmers, workers and small business owners.

2. Policy groups (“policy labs”)

Small teams of volunteers and subject experts to draft practical policy ideas on health, education, tourism, environment and livelihoods.

3. Public draft and feedback

A draft manifesto will be shared openly so that citizens can suggest changes both online and through local meetings.

4. Measurable targets

Clear 100-day, 2-year and 5-year goals for each area, with annual public progress reports that people can verify.
