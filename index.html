URP’s six core pillars

URP wants citizens to judge us by clear pillars, not just by slogans. These six pillars guide our priorities, policies and candidates.

1. Transparent governance

Public money should be traceable. Projects, costs and timelines must be visible to citizens through simple online dashboards and periodic reports.

Public dashboards
Citizen oversight

2. Mountain-first planning

Hill regions need different solutions from plains. URP supports separate planning, budgeting and implementation focus for mountain blocks and remote areas.

Anti-migration focus
Village clusters

3. Youth empowerment

Young people should not have to leave Uttarakhand for every opportunity. Skills, entrepreneurship and local employment are central to our approach.

Skill & jobs
Local enterprises

4. Women’s safety & dignity

Markets, schools, workplaces and public transport must be safer and more supportive for women, with better infrastructure and responsive systems.

Safe spaces
Support systems

5. Rural livelihoods

Tourism, horticulture, forests and local services can create dignified livelihoods in villages, so that migration becomes a choice, not a compulsion.

Village economy
Local value chains

6. Clean and responsible politics

Candidates and office-bearers should meet basic standards of integrity, public conduct and accountability, with no space for serious criminal backgrounds.

Integrity
Public accountability

Why does Uttarakhand need a new option?

For many families, the big issues remain the same: migration, unemployment, basic services, health, schools and fragile environments. URP believes that citizens deserve a calm, honest and accountable alternative.

  • Focus on everyday issues, not just headline politics.
  • Respect for regional identity and aspirations of hill and plain regions.
  • Greater space for youth, women and professionals in decision-making.

How can you join URP?

Joining URP is a simple and transparent digital process. You can start as a supporter, volunteer or local organiser and grow your role over time.

1. Fill the online membership form

Visit the Join page and submit the Google Form with your basic details (name, mobile number, voter details, district and area).

2. Connect with the coordination team

A coordinator will connect with you over call or WhatsApp to understand your background, time availability and interest areas.

3. Join a local team

You can be part of village, ward or booth-level teams, or contribute to communication, research, events and other activities.

4. Grow your responsibility

As you contribute regularly, you may be considered for higher responsibilities in local, district or state-level structures.

Ready to participate in constructive politics? Go to the Join page and submit the form.
